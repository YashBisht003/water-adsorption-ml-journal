# JECE / Elsevier Draft Checklist

Based on the current Journal of Environmental Chemical Engineering guide for authors.

## Included In Current Draft

- Title
- Author and affiliation placeholders
- Highlights
- Graphical abstract concept
- Abstract
- Keywords
- Numbered sections
- Numbered references in square brackets
- Declaration of competing interest
- CRediT authorship contribution statement placeholder
- Data availability statement
- Acknowledgements placeholder

## Still Needed Before Submission

- Final author names, affiliations, and corresponding author details.
- A graphical abstract file, preferably TIFF, EPS, PDF, or MS Office format.
- Clean figure files and captions for:
  - Track A source-specific R2.
  - Track B validation comparison.
  - Dataset / feature / validation workflow.
  - Optional SHAP/PDP interpretation.
- Final lab-source labels if available.
- A complete cited ion-descriptor table.
- Data/code availability link.
- Funding statement.
- CRediT roles for every author.
- Consistent units and notation in tables.
- Final reference check with DOIs.

## JECE Reference Style Reminder

Use numbered citations in order of appearance:

```text
... as shown previously [1,2].
Hafsa et al. [1] reported ...
```

References should include author names, article title, journal title, year,
volume/article number or pages, and DOI where available.
